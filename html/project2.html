Cooking

Growing up, I enjoyed helping my mom in the kitchen. After she had my sister, she stayed home while I was in elementary school. I remember that on any holiday or special occasion, I always had something to bring to school to share with my class. At the time, we were living in Germany, and the classes were small, about 15 people. For Halloween, it was cat cookies; Earth Day was dirt cakes; my birthday was strawberry cupcakes; when I ran for secretary, she made shortbread cookies with my face on them; and for Christmas, it was always cookies. During middle school, I fell in love with helping her with everything. I wanted to cook dinner, make sweet tea for the fridge, and bake all the time. My favorite shows were on the Cooking Channel and Gordon Ramsay. I was so in love with cooking that I wanted to go to culinary school. When *The Princess and the Frog* came out, I wanted to dream big and own a restaurant. I wanted to know how to do everything, from sushi to chocolate to Michelin-star cuisine. I couldn’t pick one cuisine. *Ratatouille* was eye-opening for learning about flavor profiles and working with different people and ideas. In middle school, I went through a heavy tea phase, and with tea you need a sweet treat, so I would bake. Anytime I felt stressed, bored, emotionally unbalanced, missed a parent while they were deployed, or wanted to bond with my little sister, I would bake. My love language is food. So my vision drifted to owning a café, where I could balance everything, tea, baked goods, delicious food, and a comforting atmosphere. It was my dream. Then something crazy happened when I was 15, the entire world shut down. COVID-19 was a virus that changed the restaurant industry forever. Small businesses closed, people stopped socializing, and the world became disconnected. We had to learn how to be separate. That goes against everything I believe the food industry is meant for, it is here to unite people through taste and experience. So my passion shifted, I was taking an AP Computer Science class and during that time I fell in love with computers. Cooking became the part of the day I look forward, and culinary school can happen at a later time in life.

Music

Starting with my grandmother, music has always been a part of my life. She played the ukulele, accordion, harp, piano, bagpipes, guitar, and organ, and eventually, she sang as well. My mother grew up singing in choirs, and as a kid, I fell in love with Disney music, which opened the door for me to experience so many different genres through my family. My grandfather loved Metallica, Avenged Sevenfold, Bonnie Tyler, and Lynyrd Skynyrd, while my grandmother loved Elvis, Prince, and The Ronettes. My mom loved Reba, Imagine Dragons, Pink, and Matisyahu. Because of all of these influences, I was exposed to a wide range of music growing up, and that variety helped shape the way I listen and connect with songs today. During junior high, I did theatre with all my friends, and that is when showtunes entered my life and I never really looked back. Music became more than something I listened to; it became something that connected me to family, friends, memories, and identity.

One song that has always stood out to me is “Vienna” by Billy Joel because it reminds me that it is okay to slow down and enjoy life. Trying to be perfect all the time can wear a person down, and I have learned that doing your best matters more than being flawless. Failing does not mean you are not improving; sometimes it is actually a sign of progress. That is easier to say than to practice, but recently I have been trying to be more consistent in everything I do and give myself more grace along the way. Music has helped me understand that growth takes time, and that reminder has been important to me.

The table below showcases my extensive theatre experience, which has been a significant part of my life. I started in junior high after we just moved to Alabama and I wanted to make new friends. I participated in two shows during that time even though I wanted to be on stage a little bit to be with my friends, I fell in love with all things tech instead. I was the only one in my theatre class who took on the set building project and also helped with showchoir crew. My self-confidence kind of wavered until one of my teachers pulled me aside and told me, "Actors without crew are just naked people standing in the dark." She explained that just because I wasn't on stage, it didn't mean I wasn't part of the show—I was just as important as the actors. That moment was a turning point for me and helped me embrace my role in theatre.Starting in high school, I was in every show I could possibly do. Despite taking AP classes, I made the time, and during that span I had the opportunity to work on a total of 28 shows. I competed in Trumbauer, which is a state competition for high school theatre, I had to oppurnity to compete three of my four years, and won top three in state stage managing all three years. I was part of Theatre Cabinet leadership, belonged to Thespian Troupe #6278, and served as Thespian President my senior year. I helped organize two trips to Thespian Festival and ran playbill ads with the cabinet to raise money for a new theatre curtain. From stage managing and assistant directing to house managing, lighting, costume design, prop design, set design, blocking, and choreography, I have done it all.I had to gouge someone's eyes out with a spoon. We did King Lear, in the round, as my Director called it. We set up bleachers, in a circle and blocked around the circle. The audience was told to sit anywhere and the show would happen around it. We lined the edges of with a black curtain, and when it became time they tilted the actor back. Through the curtain, and he wore a headband he put over his eye, and using the masking tape ball with tissue dipped in vasloine. I dippped in fake bloood, and volia. I would never change that experience for anything, and I am so grateful for all the opportunities and memoriies I created during my time. I had the ability to work with so many talented people, and I learned so much about myself and the world through theatre.
