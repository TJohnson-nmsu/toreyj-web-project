Heart of Nerd

I chose this title because it helps incorporate the different aspects of my life and tells a little bit of my story. Growing up as a military kid taught me how to adapt to new places, new routines, and new people, and that experience made me more open to exploring the world around me. Because I moved around and had to adjust so often, I learned how to be curious, flexible, and willing to make the best out of changing situations. When I was ten years old, I picked the gamertag “Nerdlife” for my Nintendo DS, and “Nerdhaven” is a dedication to that part of my identity. It reflects the side of me that enjoys technology, learning, and creating things that feel personal and meaningful. This page is also dedicated to showcasing my work in ICT 280 and showing how much I have learned throughout the class. I did not put as much effort into my last page, but I have been working hard to make this one better, more organized, and more complete. This felt the best way to bring together my past, my interests, and the work I have done in this course.

    The image of the Hobbit door was sourced from Unsplash, the photgrapher is John Thomas.

    Trying to decide on an image to represent my home page was harder than expected. Did I use a picture of my favorite Doctor Who character? Do I use a picture of myself? Do I use a picture of my favorite flower? My mind settled on my new tattoo, which I got last year on my twenty-first birthday. It is a passage from the book The Lord of the Rings. It reads as follows: “Home is behind, the world ahead… And there are many paths to tread… Through shadows to the edge of night… Until the stars are all alight” (J.R.R. Tolkien, The Fellowship of the Ring). The quote is written in the clouds over a field with mushrooms leading into the mountains.

    I remember the first time I watched The Lord of the Rings with my dad. We spent three days sitting on the couch watching the extended editions of each movie. When Bilbo Baggins first appears on screen, all he does is protest about not wanting to leave home. At the time, I was getting ready to start high school, and everything in my life felt like it was changing around me.I decided to use the door to a hobbit house as my image, representing that idea of leaving the comfort of the Shire and stepping into the unknown world ahead.
